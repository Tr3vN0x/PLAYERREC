The Rain Never Stops in Los Santos

The neon lights of Vinewood don't shine like they used to. As the city slips further into the shadows of the 2020s, the streets whisper stories of those left behind. The glamour is gone, replaced by a cold, industrial silence that stretches from the docks of La Puerta to the peaks of Mount Chiliad.

"In this city, you're either the hunter or the forgotten."

There is no glory in the grind anymore. The cars are faster, the heists are bigger, but the hearts are colder. Los Santos remains a playground for the wicked, a concrete cemetery where dreams go to die under the flickering hum of a broken street lamp.
